TOXIC STRESS WARNING

This chemical color is linked to cellular damage, stomach lining irritation, and liver inflammation in lab studies.

DYE-SAFE PUBLIC HEALTH ADVISORY

Chemical Toxicity Report

Common Name: Allura Red AC
IUPAC Name: Disodium 6-hydroxy-5-((2-methoxy-5-methyl-4-sulfonatophenyl)azo)naphthalene-2-sulfonate
Origins: Coal Tar / Petroleum Derivative
Risk Level: HIGH RISK

Associated Health Risks:

  • Triggers hyperactivity, impulsivity, and ADHD symptoms in young children.
  • May contain carcinogenic contaminants such as p-Cresidine.
  • Causes hypersensitivity, hives, and breathing difficulties in asthma patients.

Global Regulatory Status

🇪🇺 EU Warning Label Required
🇺🇸 USA FDA Approved
🌐 Other Restricted in UK

In the European Union, products containing this dye must carry the warning label: "May have an adverse effect on activity and attention in children."

Healthy Organic Swap

You can achieve this same beautiful color using natural, vitamin-rich organic plant sources!

Recommended Natural Dye: Organic Beetroot Juice / Powder
Recipe: Simmer grated fresh beetroot in water, strain, and use the concentrated juice. For powdered icing, blend freeze-dried beetroot powder with organic powdered sugar.
Health Benefits: Rich in antioxidants, nitrate compounds for blood circulation, and contains zero synthetic additives.
